The Peugeot family of industrialists and engineers, dating back to the 15th century, maintains its leading position in company still. The family company "Peugeot Brothers and Sons" in the second half of the 19th century produced near the traditional for her iron products, bicycles and parts for crews.

In 1889, Leon Serpollet built the first steam car for the company, quite successful, taking participation in the steam car race along the Paris-Lyon route. But the head of the bicycle company Armand Peugeot, who was friends with Emile Levassor, one of the pioneers of the automobile industry in France, With his help, he met G. Daimler and built his first car with gasoline Daimler engine in 1891. Although the model was not particularly original, its success inspired Arman to create a whole series next year, consisting of 10 different types of machines. One of cars participated in the Paris-Brest-Paris bicycle race in the same year and attracted everyone's attention. This proved to the fledgling automaker that an important part of commercial car racing can become a success.

In 1892, Peugeot received the most interesting order - to build an elegant car for an Algerian bey with a 4-cylinder engine and a body made of cast silver (this unique model is the decoration of the company's museum). The Peugeot car took part in the world's first automobile race, the Paris-Rouen "great run". July 22, 1894, arriving second after the steam train De Dion-Bouton ("De Dion-Brunka").

Peugeot at the beginning of the 20th century, in line with the trends of that time, strives to create a cheap car, as simple, reliable and adapted to urban conditions as possible. For these purposes, the company is invited outstanding engineer and designer E. Bugatti. Such a prototype of the "people's car" was created by him Bebe Peugeot ("Baby Peugeot"). But another direction in the company's activities was not forgotten - racing and record cars. The Peugeot Goux 1913 model develops a speed of 187 km / h, an absolute record for those years The company was the first in France to introduce conveyor assembly in these years, buying the rights to it the method of engineer E.
